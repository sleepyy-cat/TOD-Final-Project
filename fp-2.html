If things go wrong (individual team member(s) or the entire team doesn't meet one or more goals), we will schedule an emergency meeting as soon as possible to discuss how to get back on track. We will discuss every goal that was not met (while focusing on the problem and not assigning individual blame) and adjust our plan to accommodate for the delay. We will try not to push back deadlines, but rather spread out missed work among team members and tackle the problem together that same week. We recognize this will lead to a temporarily increased workload, but snowballing delays is worse in the long run. If an individual is struggling to meet their goal, we will discuss the struggles they are facing, try to resolve them on the spot, and divide the task among remaining team members (while possibly reassigning other tasks for that week to the team member) if the struggles cannot be resolved. Larger struggles among multiple team members will be the focus of these emergency meetings, so that we can all put our heads together to find a solution.
