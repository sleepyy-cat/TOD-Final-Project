Script

This project writeup, including many graphs and a scrollytelling narrative, focuses on the big decisions behind the current version of our project. The main highlight is the story that we're trying to tell, along with the questions that still remain open. As previously stated, we mainly focus on one demographic--lower-income residents--and consider how they're impacted by TOD.

The first part introduces the question and summarizes main takeaways of our project. This first chart demonstrates the proportion of TOD within all forms of housing development, thus proving the point that TOD is not yet widespread in overall housing development. As we scroll down, we consider who benefits (or doesn't benefit) from TOD, and use areas with more concentrated TOD to illustrate our concern about how lower-income residents may be impacted.

Our main visualization, the scrollytelling chloropleth map, dynamically displays the relationship between transit access and housing development, for both the state of Massachusetts overall and for zoomed in neighborhoods in the Greater Boston area. It further backs the claim that TOD is not as rampant as it possibly should be, since a lot of development is not centered on public transportation.

Finally, we explore the demographic impact of TOD and non-TOD, by factoring income and education into the analysis, since those two are often associated with determining whether populations are more or less susceptible to negative impact by TOD.

We also have a separate playground page for our key demos so that users can explore them more on their own time.
