Project Pitch

Question

Potential questions:
  1. To what extent does the MBTA provide equitable access to opportunities and essential locations such as hospitals, workplaces, and grocery stores across different demographics in Boston?
  2. How has the MBTA network shaped the population density and development patterns of Boston's municipalities?
  3. Where are the gaps in the MBTA network relative to Boston's distribution of resources?

Wireframing

Potential Question 1 Wireframe

  1. Map of Boston area, show clusters of opportunities and essential locations (interactive?): Explain that this is the primary purpose of having transit networks
  2. Pivot to data demographics of people in Boston area: Who are the people most in need of good transit? Low income, no cars, elderly, etc.
  3. Visualization of travel distance/ease of access to locations for these people: Highlight good/bad regions, along with regions representing previously-identified in-need people

Potential Question 2 Wireframe

  1. Show general population density/development changes across a time period: Map of Boston, show population using density/shading graph; explain why this matters--more dense regions could need more housing, other regions could indicate resource scarcity that needs to be addressed, etc.
  2. Add MBTA lines/stops to the map to supplement population changes: Identify any initially visible patterns (i.e. dense population regions have more stops, etc.)

Potential Question 3 Wireframe

  1. Map of Boston area, show resource distribution (i.e. hospitals) in a potentially-interactive visualization: Explain that this is the primary purpose of having transit networks
  2. Put emphasis on gaps in MBTA network (region with far-apart stops or none at all): Combine gaps with nearby resource distribution to demonstrate why this is an issue
  3. Show details (why these gaps matter, using numerical data such as travel time or cost)
